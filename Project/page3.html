Artistic Movements
Movement Time Period

Romanticism

A style of art featuring a lot of emotion, dramatic events, triumph and sometimes even terror. During the 1800s not all artists liked the neoclassical style of noble images, some artist created very dramatic events and exotic subjects and this new style was called romanticism. Romantic artists do not always paint happy scenes but aim to show great emotion in their works. Some common themes in romanticism are landscapes and battle scenes.
Characteristics:
Dramatic effects, Great expressions, Drama, Colors and lighting, Dramatic Lighting, Nature, Tension/Darkness, Wild Landscapes, Individualism

Impressionism

A style of art in which artists aim to capture atmospheric effects in their art. In 1874 a set of painters formed a new group so that they can exhibit their works independently of the academy salon that year. Their new style was called impressionism but critics did not give good reviews of their work. Instead of painting in their studio, they went outdoors and captured atmospheric effects in their work. They did this by using small dabs or stroke of paint, that when viewed from a distance blend together. An Art Critic said at the time that these painting are not complete and they look more like an impression or a sketch and this is how the name of the style came about.
Characteristics:
Brush Strokes, Plein-air painting, Lighting effects, Lines, Lighting, Colors, Subjects

German Expressionism

Style of Art which showed anguish, despair, and the deepest of emotions. German Artist wanted to express themselves through art, they were called German Expressionists and they worked after world war one in 1920s. Artists like Kathe Kollwize wanted a way to express deepest of her emotions, and she used her arts to protest against heart ship the poor around the world war one.
Characteristics:
Anguish, despair, emotions, protest against war, simple forms, vivid colors, expressive gestures or brushwork, spirituality

Cubism

Art of showing 3-D items from multiple angels on a flat canvas. Artist used object but twisted them a bit so they aim to show objects from multiple angels all at the same time. Their subject was people from everyday life. The Cubist movement started to bring diversity to the art world and some people liked it and were able to relate to it and others did not. They drew parts from multiple angles and arranged them differently to form what they thought was the most interesting composition.
Characteristics:
Multiple perspectives, geometric shapes, flattened picture plane, shapes, angels

Futurism

Futurism was aradical art movement that celebrated the modern world of machines, speed, and technology in the early 20th century. Which strongly rejected traditional forms and embraced the energy and dynamism of modern technology.
Characteristics:
Youth, technology, dynamism, speed, brutality, industrial developments like motor vehicles, aircraft, and large industrial cities

Dadaism

Art movement in which artists ridiculed contemporary culture and traditional art forms. Dada is a name for a movement of Art where there was a reaction against the traditional art. Ordinary Object were portrayed in absurd ways.
Characteristics:
Illogical and absurd, "Readymades": everyday objects as art, collages and disturbing photomontages

Surrealism

Style in Art which artists were inspired by dreams, fantasy and the subconscious mind. These artists were not interested in control/logic. Surrealist Artists developed the idea that ordinary objects should be opened up to all of the imagination.
Characteristics:
Painting were imaginative, exaggerated forms, symbolism, the element of fantasy, the use of the visual form to express and translate the unconscious, eccentric, shocking, and mysterious

Pop Art

Style of Art which portrays images from popular cultures. This style started in England, but it really became explored in America in the 1960s. This style of art Produced: paintings, sculptures, installations
Characteristics:
Super large size works, repetition in unique way, everyday Imagery, bold colors, humor
