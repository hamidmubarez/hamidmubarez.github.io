Leonardo Da-Vinci

Leonardo da Vinci (1452-1519) was an Italian polymath of the Renaissance era, renowned for his versatility in various disciplines such as painting, sculpture, science, anatomy, engineering, and architecture. Leonardo's artistic talents are epitomized by iconic works such as "The Last Supper" and "Mona Lisa." "The Last Supper" is a mural painting depicting the moment Jesus announces that one of his disciples will betray him, while the "Mona Lisa" is a portrait widely acclaimed for its enigmatic smile and masterful use of sfumato (a technique that creates a blurred, atmospheric effect).

Pablo Picasso

Pablo Picasso was a prolific and versatile artist who revolutionized 20th-century art with his innovations in painting, sculpture, and collage. Much of Picasso's work of the late 1910s and early 1920s is in a neoclassical style, and his work in the mid-1920s often has characteristics of Surrealism. His later work often combines elements of his earlier styles. There's many words to describe the art and life of Pablo Picasso. Some of those words include: Revolutionary, conflicted, innovative, complex, and productive. Exploring Artistic Options Meaning and Statements Pioneering Productivity

Claude Monet

Oscar-Claude Monet was a French painter and founder of impressionist painting who is seen as a key precursor to modernism, especially in his attempts to paint nature as he perceived it. Claude Monet was a pioneer of Impressionism, a style of painting that captured the fleeting effects of light and color on natural scenes. He left a lasting impact on the art world, influencing generations of artists who admired his vision, creativity, and mastery of light.

Marcel Duchamp

Marcel Duchamp, (born July 28, 1887, Blainville, France—died October 2, 1968, Neuilly) Henri-Robert-Marcel Duchamp was a French painter, sculptor, chess player, and writer whose work is associated with Cubism, Dada, and conceptual art. His preferred style was Dadaism Marcel Duchamp. Duchamp has had an immense impact on twentieth-century and twenty first-century art; and he had a seminal influence on the development of conceptual art. By World War I, he had rejected the work of many of his fellow artists (such as Henri Matisse) as " retinal " art, intended only to please the eye.

Salvador Dali

Salvador Dali was a Spanish surrealist painter, known for his bizarre and imaginative images that explored the subconscious mind. Salvador Dalí was born on 11 May 1904, in the town of Figueres, in the Empordà region, close to the French border in Catalonia, Spain. Surrealist Style of Art In the period from 1950 to 1970, Dalí painted many works with religious themes, though he continued to explore erotic subjects, to represent childhood memories, and to use themes centring on his wife, Gala. Notwithstanding their technical accomplishments, those later paintings are not as highly regarded as the artist’s earlier works. The most interesting and revealing of Dalí’s books is The Secret Life of Salvador Dalí (1942).

Chuck Close

Chuck Close, in full Chuck Thomas Close, (born July 5, 1940, Monroe, Washington, U.S.—died August 19, 2021, Oceanside, New York), American artist noted for his highly inventive techniques used to paint the human face. He is best known for his large-scale Photo-realist portraits. Chuck Close was an American artist known for his large-scale Photorealist portraits. Style of Art Photorealism Chuck Close is globally renowned for reinvigorating the art of portrait painting from the late 1960s to the present day, an era when photography had been challenging painting's former dominance in this area, and succeeding in steadily gaining critical appreciation as an artistic medium in its own right.

Andy Warhol

Andy Warhol was an American visual artist, film director, producer, and leading figure in the pop art movement. His works explore the relationship between artistic expression, advertising, and celebrity culture that flourished by the 1960s, and span a variety of media, including painting, silkscreening, photography, film, and sculpture. Some of his best-known works include the silkscreen paintings Campbell's Soup Cans (1962) and Marilyn Diptych (1962). Andy Warhol was a pop art pioneer who transformed everyday objects and celebrities into colorful, iconic images. He left behind a vast legacy of art and influence, as well as a museum in his hometown of Pittsburgh that showcases his work and archives
